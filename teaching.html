Teaching Philosophy

I strive to create an inclusive and engaging learning environment in which students develop statistical reasoning through real-world data, active learning, and computational tools. My teaching emphasizes conceptual understanding, clear communication, and reproducible analysis using R and other modern statistical software.

Course Development

I develop instructional materials that connect statistical concepts with computational practice and real-world data. My teaching emphasizes active learning, reproducible analysis, statistical communication, and project-based experiences.

  • Designed and developed STAT 244SC — Computational Statistics at Mount Holyoke College and served as its inaugural instructor
  • Developed R-based laboratory assignments using real-world health datasets
  • Developed NHANES-based projects for teaching statistical methods
  • Created reproducible analysis workflows using R Markdown
  • Developed semester-long research projects and instructional materials for MATH 6940 — Project in Computational Mathematics
  • Developed project-based instructional materials for introductory statistics

Student Mentoring

I mentor students through project-based learning in statistics, data science, and computational mathematics. Students work with real-world datasets while developing skills in statistical programming, visualization, modeling, reproducible research, and scientific communication.

  • Mentor undergraduate and graduate student research projects
  • Supervise computational mathematics and data-analysis projects
  • Guide students in statistical programming using R
  • Support students interested in graduate study in statistics and biostatistics
