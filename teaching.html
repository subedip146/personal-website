Teaching Philosophy

I strive to create an inclusive and engaging learning environment in which students develop statistical reasoning through real-world data, active learning, and computational tools. My teaching emphasizes conceptual understanding, clear communication, and reproducible analysis using R and other modern statistical software.

Course Development

I actively develop course materials that integrate statistical theory with computational practice and real-world data. My instructional materials emphasize hands-on learning, reproducible research, and project-based experiences that prepare students for graduate study and careers in statistics, data science, and quantitative research.

  • Designed R-based laboratory assignments using real-world datasets
  • Developed NHANES-based projects for teaching statistical methods
  • Created reproducible analysis workflows using R Markdown
  • Designed semester-long computational mathematics research projects
  • Developed project-based learning activities for introductory statistics

Student Mentoring

I enjoy mentoring students in statistics, data science, and computational mathematics through project-based learning and collaborative research. I encourage students to apply statistical methods to real-world problems, develop reproducible analytical workflows, and communicate their findings effectively.

  • Faculty mentor for undergraduate research projects
  • Supervise computational mathematics and data analysis projects
  • Guide students in statistical programming using R
  • Support students interested in graduate study in statistics and biostatistics
